# Outreach Templates for Music Industry Revolution Projects

## Overview

This document provides email templates, pitch deck outlines, and communication guidelines for reaching out to stakeholders for Studio Pass, Dimensional Knob (formerly Dimensional Radio), The Scouting Label, and Fusion Factory.

**Important**: Customize these templates for each specific project and recipient. These are starting points, not final copy.

- Public now: Studio Pass, Dimensional Knob (formerly Dimensional Radio), Fusion Factory
- Deferred/private framing: The Scouting Label
- Cross-project mentions: conditional only when ROI/risk clarity improves
- "Investor" includes non-financial execution enablers (authority, institutional access, permits/compliance leverage, ops/security/logistics support)

## General Principles

### Email Best Practices
- **Subject Line**: Clear, specific, intriguing (not clickbait)
- **Opening**: Immediate value proposition or connection
- **Body**: Brief explanation, key benefits, specific ask
- **Closing**: Clear next steps and easy response
- **Length**: 150-300 words maximum
- **Tone**: Professional but not stuffy, enthusiastic but not desperate

### Personalization
- Research recipient before reaching out
- Reference specific work or achievements
- Explain why they specifically are a good fit
- Avoid generic mass email feel

### Follow-up Strategy
- Wait 7-10 days before first follow-up
- Maximum 2-3 follow-ups
- Add new information with each follow-up
- Respect non-responses as "no"

## Pitch Deck Outline Template

### Slide Structure (10-15 slides)

1. **Cover Slide**
   - Project name and tagline
   - Your name and contact

2. **The Problem**
   - Clear, relatable problem statement
   - Market size of the problem
   - Why it matters now

3. **The Solution**
   - Your product/service
   - How it solves the problem
   - Key differentiators

4. **How It Works**
   - User flow or process diagram
   - Key features
   - Screenshots/mockups if available

5. **Market Opportunity**
   - Total addressable market (TAM)
   - Serviceable addressable market (SAM)
   - Target market initially

6. **Business Model**
   - Revenue streams
   - Pricing strategy
   - Unit economics

7. **Traction** (if applicable)
   - Users, revenue, partnerships
   - Key metrics and growth
   - Notable achievements

8. **Competition**
   - Competitive landscape
   - Your differentiation
   - Sustainable advantages

9. **Go-to-Market Strategy**
   - Customer acquisition approach
   - Marketing channels
   - Strategic partnerships

10. **Team**
    - Founders and key team members
    - Relevant experience
    - Advisors

11. **Financial Projections**
    - 3-5 year revenue projections
    - Key assumptions
    - Path to profitability

12. **The Ask**
    - What you're seeking (investment, partnership, etc.)
    - How funds will be used
    - Expected outcomes

13. **Contact**
    - Your contact information
    - Website, demo links
    - Social media

## Communication Guidelines

### Do's
- ✅ Research recipient thoroughly first
- ✅ Personalize every communication
- ✅ Be specific about what you're asking
- ✅ Make it easy to say yes (specific times, clear action)
- ✅ Respect people's time (brief, focused)
- ✅ Follow up appropriately (not too aggressive)
- ✅ Express genuine enthusiasm
- ✅ Thank people for their time

### Don'ts
- ❌ Send generic mass emails
- ❌ Make unrealistic claims or promises
- ❌ Be pushy or desperate
- ❌ Over-explain in initial outreach
- ❌ Neglect to proofread
- ❌ Send without clear call-to-action
- ❌ Follow up too frequently
- ❌ Bad-mouth competitors

## Success Metrics

Track your outreach effectiveness:

- **Response Rate**: Target 20-30% for warm outreach, 5-10% for cold
- **Conversion Rate**: Responses to meetings: 50-70%
- **Time to Response**: Average 3-7 days
- **Follow-up Success**: Second emails often get 10-15% additional responses

Continuously refine based on what works for your specific audience.
